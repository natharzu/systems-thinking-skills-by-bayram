# Decision Document: Personal OKR Abandonment

**Problem Statement (Apex):** Most personal OKRs get abandoned by week 3.

---

## Verdict & Constraint Located

### **System Constraint: Policy & Behavior**
The primary system constraint is the policy of **framing OKRs as abstract lagging outcomes without establishing event-based tracking habits and calendar-blocked review rituals**. 

*   **Status:** Located. No additional data is needed to verify this policy gap; it requires structural process and behavioral design changes.
*   **Weakest Support Link (Hypothesis):** Users mistake the emotional satisfaction of planning for actual progress (`R0_L2`). If this neurobiological planning satisfaction is *not* the driver of abandonment, the constraint shifts to **dopamine asymmetry / lack of immediate incentives** (`R2`).

---

## What to Do

1.  **Reframe Goal Design:** Transition personal OKRs from lagging outcome aspirations (e.g., "Read 12 books this quarter") to weekly behavioral lead metrics with explicit trigger-action plans (e.g., "If it is 10 PM, I will open my current book on my nightstand and read for 10 minutes").
2.  **Establish Automatic Tracking Rituals:** Block a recurring 10-minute slot on the calendar for weekly reviews, integrating tracking directly into existing daily digital workflows (e.g., calendar invites, phone alarms) rather than relying on ad-hoc willpower.

---

## What NOT to Do (Negative Branches)

> [!WARNING]
> Avoid these common fixes as they are likely to backfire:
>
> 1.  **Forcing complex OKR software or rigid daily update reminders:** This increases cognitive friction and accelerates abandonment due to tool fatigue (the tools are not the bottleneck).
> 2.  **Adding high-stakes public shaming or severe negative consequences for missing goals:** This prompts users to set extremely low, unchallenging targets to ensure safety, gaming the metrics without achieving real growth.

---

## The Single Cheapest Test (To Fork-Decide)

### **Test #1: Trigger-Action Cue Trial**
*   **Description:** Run a 2-week trial where a cohort of users set OKRs but are randomly split: Group A sets standard outcome goals; Group B sets explicit event-based habit cues (implementation intentions).
*   **Decides:** Adjudicates whether explicit trigger-action cues significantly reduce week-3 abandonment rates compared to standard outcome framing.

### **Test #2: Planning Motivation Correlation Survey**
*   **Description:** Survey users post-goal-setting to measure self-reported motivation/satisfaction levels, then correlate these scores with their subsequent weekly tracking frequency.
*   **Decides:** Adjudicates whether front-loaded planning satisfaction correlates with earlier abandonment (adjudicating the load-bearing hypothesis `R0_L2`).
